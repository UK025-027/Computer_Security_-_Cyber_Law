Introduction to Information Security

Information security, often called InfoSec, is the practice of protecting information so that it remains safe, accurate, and accessible to the right people. Information can exist in many forms — digital files stored on computers, data in cloud systems, printed documents, or even recorded media. No matter the form, the main goal of information security is to protect the value and reliability of that information.


Introduction to the CIA Triad

The CIA Triad is a fundamental model used in information security to guide policies and practices for protecting data. It provides a simple but effective framework for understanding how information should be secured. In this context, CIA does not refer to the intelligence agency. Instead, it represents three core principles: Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability. These three components together form the foundation of modern information security systems.

Confidentiality

Confidentiality ensures that information is accessible only to authorized individuals. Sensitive data such as passwords, personal records, financial details, or official documents must be kept private. Methods such as encryption, user authentication, and access controls are used to maintain confidentiality. When confidentiality is properly maintained, only the intended users can view the information.

Integrity

Integrity means that information must remain accurate, complete, and trustworthy. Data should not be changed, modified, or deleted without proper authorization. Maintaining integrity ensures that information can be relied upon for decision-making. For example, academic records, legal documents, and financial statements must remain correct and unaltered unless officially updated.

Availability

Availability ensures that information and systems are accessible to authorized users whenever needed. It ensures that data is not only protected but also usable. Systems should function properly so that users can retrieve information without unnecessary delays. Backups, maintenance, and proper system management help ensure availability.

Together, confidentiality, integrity, and availability form the foundation of information security. These principles guide how organizations design policies and manage data effectively. Information security is ultimately about keeping information private, accurate, and accessible only to the right people at the right time.
